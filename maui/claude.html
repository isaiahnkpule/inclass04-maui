Summer

An ocean, which was so still it looked glossy rather than the water itself, until being touched. A simple ripple would spread for yards until fading away having the stage of the ocean all to itself. The day was full of relaxation, storytelling, and kanikapila (an impromptu Hawaiian jam session). Later that day as the sun was setting a whale decided to come around our idle drift in the ocean. Since the water was as still as it was, we could spot the incoming whale from a while away. As it stayed with us through the rest of the evening as we head into the night, we started to notice not only the light on the night sky but also the ocean. As the whale caused movements in the ocean, bioluminescent planktons lit up the ocean all within a 30-yard diameter from us. It was simply magical. A vast amount of stars lit up the night sky, making it nearly impossible to depict constellation. The cluttered sky gleamed with lights of various sizes and colors. The Milky Way galaxy itself made an appearance that night. It all came to an end the following day as the wind began to pick up mid-afternoon.
